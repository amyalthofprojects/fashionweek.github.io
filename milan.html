Gucci

1 / 6

Alessandro Michele isn't one of those designers who cleans the slate each season and comes up with a completely new idea. Rather, he keeps building upon the DNA he's created for Gucci ever since taking over the house in January 2015. His new signatures — things like florals, kooky sunglasses, unexpected layering, brocades, and glitter — are evergreen and seasonless, and every six months, Michele continues to push them one step further.

Prada

2 / 6

This is a particularly political moment for fashion, on two levels. Outward-facing, the industry continues to reflect upon and react to the realities of the Trump era and its global ramifications. Looking inward, fashion considers its interface with major ideological issues — diversity, gender fluidity, cultural appropriation, body image.

Moschino

3 / 6

What Jeremy Scott brings to the catwalk for Moschino is a playful and irreverent collection that expresses the desire for lightness and not taking things seriously during this historic moment and current international political context. Positivity jumps out in the form of shirts and dresses printed with the famous My Little Pony characters, all pastel and rainbow, worn with contrasting leather biker jackets and fetish accessories. The tutu inspired tulle skirts alternate with black leather hot-pants, while the final outfits are fabric transpositions of colorful three-dimensional flowers that just blossomed, spectacular multicolour bouquets.

Versace

4 / 6

To mark the 20th anniversary of Gianni Versace’s passing, Donatella Versace dedicated the Spring 2018 collection to her brother. Held at the Triennale Museum in Milan, the show made fashion history. From start to finish, the collection was an ode to the late designer’s mastery for bold femininity, outlandish splashes of print, and unapologetic glamour.

Fendi

5 / 6

Italian futurism meets the Tropics and injects Fendi Spring/Summer 2018 collection with colour and energy. Tapping into the skilled craftsmanship of the artisans working for the double F Roman maison, Karl Lagerfeld and Silvia Venturini Fendi elevate every fabric and every material turning it into something precious and unique. Tropical flowers are reworked into inlay embellishments on long fur pieces, check patterns take over the entire surface of pencil skirts, arty stripes add colour to suits while logos are turned into pop graffiti on urban style bomber jackets.

Max Mara

6 / 6

Never mind the Max Mara show notes that went on about “Baudelairean views” and “a nouvelle vague of philosophers-cum-gardeners.” The spring 2018 collection was straightforward: a series of tailored silhouettes, minimalist and Nineties derived, repeated in seven or eight color ways and a print. There were a few variations here and there but the mainstays were slim transparent blazers and pants; pencil trench skirts; sharp, lean jumpsuits; jean jackets, and sporty tanks worn with belts slung with long panels of fabric that made a skirt-like contraption.
